# Fix three classification errors identified in issue #3354

## Summary

This PR addresses three classification errors in Uberon that were causing unsatisfiability issues when merging with ZFA. All three issues stem from incorrect classification of material structures as immaterial entities.

## Changes Made

### 1. uvea (UBERON:0001768)
**Change:** Removed incorrect `part_of` relationship to 'anterior segment of eyeball' (UBERON:0001801)

**Rationale:** 
- The uvea extends both anteriorly and posteriorly relative to the lens
- One of its parts, the optic choroid (UBERON:0001776), overlaps the posterior segment of the eyeball
- The existing `contributes_to_morphology_of camera-type eye` relationship provides adequate hierarchical placement
- Removing this relationship resolves the logical inconsistency

### 2. future brain vesicle (UBERON:0013150)
**Change:** Reclassified from `is_a: UBERON:0010064 ! open anatomical space` to `is_a: UBERON:0000061 ! anatomical structure`

**Rationale:**
- Consistent with Uberon's convention of representing anatomical cavities/spaces using terms for the material structures that form them
- The parent term 'ventricular system of central nervous system' is explicitly defined as "a set of **structures** containing cerebrospinal fluid"
- Aligns with other similar terms like 'lumen of central canal of spinal cord' which represent the space separately from the material structure
- Makes the term classification logically consistent

### 3. scale circulus (UBERON:2002051)
**Change:** Reclassified from `is_a: UBERON:0006800 ! anatomical line` to `is_a: UBERON:0000061 ! anatomical structure`

**Rationale:**
- The definition states it is a "fine ridge on surface of scale", indicating it has physical substance
- ZFA (the source ontology) correctly classifies this as a material anatomical structure
- A ridge must have material consistency to be observable
- Aligns Uberon with ZFA's classification
