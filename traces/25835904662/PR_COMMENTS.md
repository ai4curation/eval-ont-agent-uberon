## Rationale

The issue requested a term to represent the combined intima + media region
of the carotid artery wall. The term is needed in OBA to define the
phenotype "carotid intima-media thickness" (cIMT), a widely used
ultrasound-based vascular biomarker (see PMID:39416432, "Plasma proteomics
and carotid intima-media thickness in the UK biobank cohort", Chen et al.).

The term is modelled as a genus/differentia composite: it is a
multi-tissue structure that is part of the carotid artery segment and
has both the tunica intima and the tunica media as parts. It is declared
disjoint from `tunica adventitia of blood vessel`, since the
intima-media region by definition excludes the adventitia.
