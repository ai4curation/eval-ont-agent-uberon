# PR: Fix carotid body classification

## Summary
Fixed the incorrect ontological classification of the carotid body (UBERON:0001629) by moving it from the cardiovascular system to the peripheral nervous system, which accurately reflects its biological function and developmental origin.

## Problem
The carotid body was incorrectly classified with a `part_of` relationship to the "common carotid artery plus branches" (UBERON:0001530), placing it within the cardiovascular system hierarchy. This misclassification contradicted the term's own definition describing it as "a sensory organ in the peripheral nervous system."

## Solution
Modified the `part_of` relationship for UBERON:0001629:
- **Removed**: `relationship: part_of UBERON:0001530` (cardiovascular system)
- **Added**: `relationship: part_of UBERON:0000010` (peripheral nervous system)

## Scientific Justification
The carotid body:
- Is a **chemoreceptive sensory organ** that detects changes in blood pH, O₂, and CO₂
- Is **developmentally derived from neural crest** tissue
- Is **functionally part of the autonomic nervous system** (vagal afferents)
- Is **physically located** in the adventitia of the carotid artery bifurcation

While the carotid body is anatomically adjacent to the carotid artery, this does not make it part of the cardiovascular system. The existing `overlaps` relationships with the carotid arteries (UBERON:0001070 and UBERON:0001532) correctly capture this spatial adjacency.
