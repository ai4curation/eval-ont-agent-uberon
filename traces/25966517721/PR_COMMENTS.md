## Rationale

### Biological Basis
The carotid body is a sensory organ that detects changes in blood chemistry (pH, CO₂, O₂) and provides critical feedback for respiratory and cardiovascular regulation. While anatomically located adjacent to the carotid artery bifurcation, it is:

1. **Functionally**: A chemoreceptor organ of the peripheral nervous system
2. **Developmentally**: Derived from neural crest cells (like other paraganglia)
3. **Structurally**: Located in the adventitia (nerve-rich outer layer) of the artery, not as part of the arterial wall itself
