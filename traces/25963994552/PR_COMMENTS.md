## Scientific Rationale

The carotid body is a bilateral sensory organ that functions as a chemoreceptor detecting chemical variations in arterial blood, including:
- Oxygen concentration
- Carbon dioxide concentration  
- Blood pH
- Temperature

While the carotid body is anatomically located adjacent to (at the adventitia of) the bifurcation of the common carotid artery, this anatomical proximity does not make it structurally part of the cardiovascular system. The term's existing classification as a "nonchromaffin paraganglion" (UBERON:0034979) already correctly identifies it as a sensory organ.

## Evidence

According to PMID:32965908 (Kaprive & Menezes, 2023), the carotid body is explicitly described as "a 2 to 6 mm, round bilateral sensory organ in the peripheral nervous system." This classification is based on:

1. **Functional role:** The carotid body serves a sensory/chemoreceptive function in detecting blood chemistry changes
2. **Developmental origin:** It develops as a neural crest-derived sensory structure
3. **Cellular composition:** Contains chemoreceptor cells (type I and type II cells) that signal to the nervous system

The previous classification appeared to conflate anatomical location (near the carotid artery) with structural membership, leading to incorrect placement in the cardiovascular system.
