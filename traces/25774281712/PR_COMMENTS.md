## Rationale

The issue correctly identified that both neurula stage and pharyngula stage are chordate-specific concepts:

- **Neurula stage** is characterized by neural tube formation (GO:0001841), which is specific to chordates. The term is already marked as `simultaneous_with GO:0001841 ! neural tube formation` and the neural tube is a chordate-specific structure.

- **Pharyngula stage** is defined by the presence of pharyngeal arches/clefts, which are chordate-specific structures. The definition itself states "At the pharyngula stage, all vertebrate embryos show remarkable similarities" and the literature cited in the issue confirms this is vertebrate/chordate-specific.

By converting the relationship between late embryonic stage and pharyngula stage to a GCI (General Class Inclusion), we ensure that:
- Late embryonic stage remains broadly applicable to all metazoans
- The precedence relationship to pharyngula stage only applies within Chordata
- This follows the same pattern already used in the ontology (see line 20 of UBERON:0007220 with RnorDv:0000010)
