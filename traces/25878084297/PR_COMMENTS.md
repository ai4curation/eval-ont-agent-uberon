# Fix Carotid Body Classification

## Summary
This PR corrects the classification of the carotid body (UBERON:0001629) to properly place it within the peripheral nervous system rather than the cardiovascular system.

## Problem
The carotid body was incorrectly classified as `part_of` the "common carotid artery plus branches" (UBERON:0001530). While the carotid body is anatomically located adjacent to the carotid artery bifurcation (in the adventitia), it is not structurally part of the cardiovascular system.

## Solution
The carotid body is a sensory organ of the peripheral nervous system, consisting of chemoreceptive and supporting cells that detect changes in blood pH, carbon dioxide, and oxygen concentrations. Its proper classification reflects its functional role and developmental origin.

### Scientific Rationale
The carotid body is a specialized peripheral chemoreceptor organ that:
- Contains type I and type II chemoreceptor cells (properly reflected in has_part relationships)
- Functions in detection of chemical stimuli (GO:0009593)
- Plays a role in respiratory and cardiovascular regulation through neural signaling
- Is developmentally and functionally part of the peripheral nervous system
- Is located adjacent to (not within) the carotid artery structure

The previous classification based solely on anatomical proximity was misleading. This correction aligns the ontology with the functional and developmental nature of the carotid body.
