# PR: Add 5 new tooth surface terms

## Summary

This PR addresses issue #3583 by creating 5 new terms for tooth surfaces in the UBERON ontology. These terms follow FDI (Fédération Dentaire Internationale) dental classification standards and are based on well-established dental anatomy terminology.

## Terms Created

| Term ID | Name | Definition |
|---------|------|-----------|
| UBERON:9900001 | distal surface of tooth | A tooth surface that is oriented away from the median plane of the dental arch or oral cavity. |
| UBERON:9900002 | incisal surface of tooth | A tooth surface that forms the cutting edge of an incisor or canine tooth. It functions to shear or incise food during biting. |
| UBERON:9900003 | labial surface of tooth | A tooth surface that faces the lips. The term labial surface is usually used for surfaces of incisors and canines that are present just adjacent to the lips. |
| UBERON:9900004 | lingual surface of tooth | A tooth surface that faces the tongue or its anatomical equivalent. |
| UBERON:9900005 | mesial surface of tooth | A tooth surface that is oriented toward the median plane of the dental arch. |

## Design Decisions

1. **Hierarchy**: Each tooth surface is positioned as both a surface structure (general anatomical classification) and a part of teeth (functional classification). This dual inheritance provides flexibility for different use cases.

2. **Definition Sources**: Definitions were validated against:
   - HL7 FDI surface code system (https://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem-FDI-surface.html)
   - Dental Education Hub (https://dentaleducationhub.com/surfaces-of-the-teeth/)

3. **Nomenclature**: The issue comments discussed adding "facial surface" as a parent term. The approach taken here uses "facial surface of tooth" as a synonym on the labial surface term (UBERON:9900003), which aligns with the dental field where "facial" often refers to labial surfaces in anterior teeth.
