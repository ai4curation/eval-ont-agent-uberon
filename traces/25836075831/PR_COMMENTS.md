# PR: Obsolete vestigial life cycle temporal boundary terms

Partial resolution for issue #3464.

## Summary

This PR obsoletes the four "life cycle temporal boundary" terms that the issue
discussion identified as useless vestiges of over-formalization. It does **not**
attempt the broader re-parenting of `life cycle` and `life cycle stage` under
`process` (see *Scope* below).

| Term | Status | `consider` |
|------|--------|-----------|
| UBERON:0035943 `life cycle temporal boundary` | obsoleted | UBERON:0000104 (`life cycle`) |
| UBERON:0035944 `life-death temporal boundary` | obsoleted | UBERON:0000071 (`death stage`) |
| UBERON:0035945 `start of life cycle` | obsoleted | UBERON:0000106 (`zygote stage`) |
| UBERON:0035946 `start of neonate stage` | obsoleted | UBERON:0007221 (`neonate stage`) |

## Rationale

From the issue discussion:

- @cmungall (issue author): "we have 3 terms that appear to be useless vestiges
  of the heady days of over-formalization ... these strange sounding terms might
  be better colloquially called conception, birth, death. IMO 0D is overmodeling,
  we are better just treating these concepts analogous to how we treat 3D
  boundaries in uberon, as actually having some extent, albeit minimal".
- @gouttegd: confirmed that "neither 'life cycle temporal boundary' nor any of
  its 3 children are actually used anywhere within Uberon. They are also not
  used by any of the taxon-specific life-stage ontologies".

A `grep` over `uberon-edit.obo` confirms the only references to these four IDs
are within their own stanzas (parent/child relationships among the four), so no
rewiring of other terms is required.

The `consider` tags point to the corresponding life cycle stages — these are the
concepts curators are most likely to want when they encounter the obsolete IDs.

## Scope: what this PR does NOT do

The original title of #3464 is *Positioning 'life cycle' and 'life cycle stage'
under 'process'*. This part is **deliberately left alone** because:

1. There is no `process` (BFO:0000015) class currently in Uberon —
   `processual entity` (UBERON:0000000, mapped to `BFO:0000003` /
   `occurrent`) is the only upper-level class available. Pulling in
   BFO:0000015 needs separate consideration.
2. @cmungall noted "I don't think Uberon cares about upper level representation
   of this" and the most recent comment on the ticket was "We should hopefully
   reach resolution soon on the linked ticket [COB#40] then I think we can close
   this?" — i.e. the re-parenting decision is parked on COB.
3. UBERON:0035943 was the only `life cycle`-adjacent term that was both an
   unambiguous vestige and free of downstream consumers; obsoleting it is
   orthogonal to the COB decision.
