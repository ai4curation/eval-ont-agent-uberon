The change corrects the anatomical classification to reflect the carotid body's true biological nature as a sensory organ.

## Scientific Rationale

The carotid body is a neuroendocrine sensory organ that functions as a chemoreceptor in the peripheral nervous system. While anatomically positioned adjacent to the bifurcation of the common carotid artery, it is:

1. **Developmentally distinct**: Develops from neural crest tissue (like other paraganglia)
2. **Functionally neural**: Acts as a sensory receptor organ detecting pH, CO₂, and O₂ concentrations
3. **Structurally independent**: Forms a discrete organ system within the PNS, not part of the vascular system

The previous classification placed it within the cardiovascular system (as "part_of common carotid artery plus branches"), which was anatomically misleading. The carotid body is adjacent to but not structurally part of the vasculature.
