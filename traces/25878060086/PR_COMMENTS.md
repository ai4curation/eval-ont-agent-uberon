## Summary

This PR revises the definition of cardiac septum (UBERON:0002099) to be more comprehensive and accurate with respect to its child terms.

## Problem

The previous definition was:
> "The thin membranous structure between the two heart atria or the thick muscular structure between the two heart ventricles."

This definition was too narrow as it only covered:
- Septa between the two atria
- Septa between the two ventricles

However, the child terms (identified via their logical definitions with `intersection_of: UBERON:0002099`) include:
1. **Interatrial septum** (UBERON:0002085) - divides the left and right atria ✓ covered
2. **Interventricular septum** (UBERON:0002094) - separates the right and left ventricles ✓ covered
3. **Atrioventricular septum** (UBERON:0005989) - separates right atrium and left ventricle ✗ NOT covered
4. **Outflow tract septum** (UBERON:0004142) - partition in the outflow tract ✗ NOT covered
5. **Aortico-pulmonary spiral septum** (UBERON:0006207) - separates truncus arteriosus/great vessels ✗ NOT covered

## Solution

The new definition is:
> "A wall within the heart that divides cardiac chambers or subdivides the cardiac outflow tract."

This definition:
- Covers all child terms including atrioventricular and outflow tract septa
- Removes the overly specific descriptions of "thin membranous" vs "thick muscular" which don't apply uniformly to all cardiac septa
- Uses the general term "wall" which aligns with the parent term "septum" (defined as "A wall, dividing a cavity or structure into smaller ones")
- Accurately describes the function of dividing chambers and subdividing the outflow tract
