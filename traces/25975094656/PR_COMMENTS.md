## Summary
The carotid body was incorrectly asserted to be `part_of` common carotid artery plus branches (UBERON:0001530). The reporter pointed out that the carotid body is *adjacent to* but not structurally part of the common carotid artery and its branches; it should instead be classified as part of the peripheral nervous system, consistent with its function as a chemoreceptive sensory organ.

## Rationale and evidence
- The existing textual definition of UBERON:0001629 already states that the carotid body is "a small cluster of chemoreceptive and supporting cells located near the bifurcation of the common carotid artery that serves as a chemoreceptive organ" — i.e. it sits at the bifurcation but is not part of the artery itself.
- PMID:32965908 explicitly describes the carotid body as "a … bilateral sensory organ in the peripheral nervous system located in the adventitia of the bifurcation of the common carotid artery." It is anatomically embedded in the adventitia at the bifurcation, but classified as a PNS organ.
- The existing `is_a` parent (UBERON:0034979 nonchromaffin paraganglion) remains appropriate and is unchanged. Paraganglia develop from the neural crest and the nonchromaffin lineage gives rise to peripheral chemoreceptive structures, so `part_of` peripheral nervous system is internally consistent with the is_a parent.
- The existing `overlaps` relationships with the internal carotid artery (UBERON:0001532) and external carotid artery (UBERON:0001070) continue to capture the carotid body's anatomical location at the bifurcation, so location information is not lost.
