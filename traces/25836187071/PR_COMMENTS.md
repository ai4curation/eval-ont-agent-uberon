# Make organism substance disjoint from multicellular organism

Addresses issue #2421.

## Change

Added a single line to `UBERON:0000463` (`organism substance`) in `src/ontology/uberon-edit.obo`:

```
disjoint_from: UBERON:0000468 {source="https://github.com/obophenotype/uberon/issues/2421"} ! multicellular organism
```

## Rationale

The issue (raised by @ddooley and seconded by @cmungall) requests that `multicellular organism` and `organism substance` be made disjoint, so that mistakenly defining e.g. `human milk` as `milk and Homo sapiens` (where `Homo sapiens` is treated as a subclass of multicellular organism) triggers unsatisfiability instead of silently being accepted.

In @cmungall's words on the issue:

> This issue is about the request to make substance and organism disjoint. This is completely reasonable and straightforward. I am surprised we don't have this already, actually. I can't think of any exceptions. There are no organisms made of gas, liquid or other substances created by the body. Even an ingested animal ceases at some point to become a multicellular organism and is broken down into substances.
